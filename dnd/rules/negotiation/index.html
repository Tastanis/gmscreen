Benevolence

Motivation: They feel a duty to share resources, but can't give away everything. Many will help only certain people (e.g., their study group) or feel their limited supplies are better used elsewhere.

Pitfall: They believe no one deserves help just for asking‚Äîgenerosity is na√Øve.

Discovery

Motivation: They crave new knowledge‚Äîrare spells, hidden lore, or undiscovered parts of campus. Their curiosity may revolve around solving a specific mystery or simply exploring unknown magic.

Pitfall: They shun the unknown; past experiments or discoveries went badly.

Appeals: Highlight opportunities to learn or gain rare items. E.g. "Let us borrow your translation cipher for the Codex Mortis, and you can read our notes when we finish."

Freedom

Motivation: They despise authority and value independence. They might want to free themselves or others from control.

Pitfall: They think chaos would reign without authority; they might consider themselves best fit to lead.

Appeals: Show how the agreement protects or grants liberty. E.g. "Help us expose the Headmaster's corruption, and we can work together to get those harsh rules rescinded."

Greed

Motivation: They want wealth or coveted things‚Äîwhether coin, relics, or exclusive privileges. They might share only with trusted friends.

Pitfall: They reject material bribes and care more about ideals than resources.

Appeals: Offer rewards or riches in return. E.g. "Help us battle the overmind; we'll split the dragon's hoard equally with you."

Higher Authority

Motivation: They are loyal to a person, group, or deity. That authority‚Äîan archmage, mentor, or god‚Äîcomes before personal desire.

Pitfall: They scoff at serving anyone and refuse to obey orders from above.

Justice

Motivation: They want wrongdoers punished and the righteous protected, based on their sense of right and wrong.

Pitfall: They see justice as a myth; everything is conflict, and no one truly gets what they deserve.

Appeals: Align your request with their sense of fairness. E.g. "You hate cheaters; help us catch the students stealing exams, and we'll expose the professor who sells them."

Legacy

Motivation: They want lasting fame and to be remembered. Some might even seek immortality or transformation to live on through legend.

Pitfall: They consider glory-seeking a waste of time.

Appeals: Promise renown or lasting credit. E.g. "Give us your research notes, and we'll make sure everyone knows you discovered the Binding Spell."

Power

Motivation: They want more influence and control. This could be over groups, resources, or knowledge.

Pitfall: They have no interest in ruling; they might respect power but refuse to seek it themselves.

Appeals: Stress how helping you strengthens or secures their influence. E.g. "Get us into the restricted section, and we'll lobby for you to head the Student Council."

Protection

Motivation: They prioritize safeguarding people, secrets, or places. They take duty seriously, even at great personal cost.

Pitfall: They won't risk their life or belongings to protect others and expect everyone to fend for themselves.

Appeals: Show how your request protects what they care about. E.g. "Lend us your griffons for a day to send a warning; in return, we'll provide armor that keeps your nephew safe during his fieldwork."

Appeals: Offer something that makes their party plans better or frees up their fun time. E.g. "Forge five Chronokinesis Crowns for us, and we'll reward you with vintage elven wine for your next festival."
